Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar

The man who brought change.

Dr.B. R. Ambedkar explaining amendments to MR. Prime minister Jawaharlal Nehru

Here is the timeline of Dr.B.R.Ambedkar

  • 1913- B. R. Ambedkar passed the matriculation examination from Bombay University, becoming one of the first untouchable students to do so.

  • 1916- Ambedkar successfully completed his Bachelor's degree in Economics and Political Science from Bombay University.

  • 1920- he traveled to the United States to pursue further studies, and in 1927, he obtained a Doctorate in Economics from Columbia University in New York.

  • 1927- Ambedkar joined the Simon Commission as a representative of the untouchable community, but resigned shortly after due to the refusal of the commission to address the demands of the depressed classes.

  • 1930- he led the Mahad Satyagraha, a movement to challenge the ban on untouchables' access to public water sources.

  • 1932- Ambedkar played a major role in the Round Table Conferences held in London, advocating for the rights and representation of untouchables in the political system.

  • 1935- he drafted and submitted the Bahishkrit Bharat (Excluded India) proposal, which sought to improve the social status of untouchables and promote equality.

  • 1942- Ambedkar founded the All India Scheduled Castes Federation, an organization dedicated to fighting for the rights of untouchable communities.

  • 1947- he was appointed as the Law Minister of India in the interim government formed after India's independence.

  • 1950- Ambedkar played a pivotal role in drafting the Constitution of India, which included provisions to protect the rights and interests of the marginalized sections of society.

  • 1951- he founded the Buddhist Society of India and embraced Buddhism, renouncing Hinduism and encouraging the dalit community to follow suit.

  • 1952- Ambedkar was elected as a member of the Rajya Sabha, the upper house of the Indian Parliament.

  • 1955- he established the Bharatiya Bouddha Mahasabha to promote Buddhism and spread education among the dalits.

  • 1956- Ambedkar led a mass conversion ceremony in Nagpur, where he and thousands of his followers converted to Buddhism.

  • Sadly, on December 6, 1956, Ambedkar passed away, leaving behind a rich legacy of social, political, and economic reform.
